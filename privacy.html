Who we are

Visa Nest provides travel planning and visa-related administrative assistance to individuals and businesses. For the purposes of UK data protection law, Visa Nest is the controller of personal data processed as described in this policy.

Postal address: Office 211, Unit 5, Mayfair, London W1C 2BU, United Kingdom.
Email: info@visanest.co.uk
Phone: 07446 230755

Data we collect

We may collect and process the following categories of information, depending on how you interact with us:

  • Identity & contact details: name, email address, telephone number, and similar details you provide when you complete our contact form, email us, call us, or message us through approved channels.
  • Enquiry content: the subject of your request, your message, travel dates, destination preferences, nationality or residency information you choose to share, and any documents or attachments you send (where applicable).
  • Marketing preferences: if you sign up for newsletters or updates, we collect your email address and related subscription preferences.
  • Technical & usage data: IP address, browser type, device type, approximate location derived from IP, pages viewed, and timestamps. This helps us maintain security and improve the site.

We do not ask for special category data (such as health data) through our standard website forms. If you voluntarily send us sensitive information, we will only use it where we have a lawful basis and it is necessary for your request.

How we use your information

We use personal data to:

  • Respond to enquiries and provide the travel and visa assistance services you request;
  • Manage client relationships, appointments, and follow-up communication;
  • Send marketing emails where you have opted in (you can unsubscribe at any time);
  • Operate, secure, and improve our website and internal processes;
  • Comply with legal obligations, respond to regulators, and establish or defend legal claims where necessary.

Lawful bases for processing

Under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, we rely on one or more of the following bases, depending on the activity:

  • Contract — where processing is necessary to take steps at your request before entering a contract, or to perform our agreement with you.
  • Legitimate interests — for example, to run our business, improve our website, keep records, and send relevant non-marketing service messages, where those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Consent — where required for marketing communications or non-essential cookies (see below).
  • Legal obligation — where we must process data to comply with the law.

Sharing and processors

We do not sell your personal data. We may share information with:

  • Service providers who assist us with hosting, email delivery, form handling, analytics, customer relationship tools, or IT security, strictly under contract and instructions;
  • Professional advisers such as accountants or lawyers, where required;
  • Authorities when we are legally required to disclose information.

Where a processor is located outside the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards (such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement or Addendum) are in place where required.

How long we keep data

We retain personal data only for as long as needed for the purposes described above, including to satisfy legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Typical retention periods depend on the nature of the data: enquiry records may be kept for a number of years after our last interaction with you; marketing data is kept until you withdraw consent or unsubscribe; technical logs may be kept for shorter security-related periods.

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against accidental loss, unauthorised access, alteration, or disclosure. No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure; we encourage you to use strong passwords and avoid sending unnecessary sensitive documents by unsecured email.

Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Request access to your personal data;
  • Request correction of inaccurate data;
  • Request erasure, restriction of processing, or object to certain processing;
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
  • Request portability of data you provided, where technically feasible;
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in Contact us below. We may need to verify your identity before responding.

Cookies and similar technologies

Our website may use cookies or similar technologies that are strictly necessary for the site to function, and, where we deploy them, analytics or performance cookies to understand how visitors use the site. Where non-essential cookies are used, we will seek your consent in line with the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) and present choices through a suitable banner or preference centre when implemented.

International transfers

If we transfer personal data outside the UK, we will ensure an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place (for example UK adequacy regulations, standard contractual clauses, or the UK Addendum), and we will assess risks where required.

Children

Our services are not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have collected such data, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page will change when we publish a new version. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Where changes are material, we may provide additional notice (for example by email if we hold your address).

Contact us about privacy

For any questions about this policy or how we handle your personal data, email info@visanest.co.uk or write to the postal address above. You can also reach us via our contact form.

Important: This policy is provided for transparency and general information. It does not constitute legal advice. If you need advice on your specific situation, you should consult a qualified legal professional or the relevant embassy or authority.
